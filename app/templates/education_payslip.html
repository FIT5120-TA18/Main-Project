How to read your payslip

Your payslip shows how much you earned and what deductions were taken out before the money hits your bank account.

Key terms

Gross income:

Your total earnings before any deductions. This is the number your employer uses to calculate tax and super.

Tax withheld:

The amount your employer sends to the ATO (tax office) on your behalf. This is based on your gross pay and your tax-free threshold. In 26, you don't pay tax on your first $18,200 earned per year.

2025
Superannuation (super):

Your employer must contribute 11.5% of your gross pay into a super fund (as of 26). This money is held for your retirement and is separate from your regular pay. You won't see it in your bank account until you retire.

2025
Net or take-home pay:

What actually lands in your bank account each pay period. This is gross pay minus tax and any other deductions (e.g., union fees, health insurance).

Quick example

Gross weekly pay $800
Tax withheld
Superannuation (11.5%)
Net take-home pay $604

Source: ATO Pay as you go withholding, 26.2025
